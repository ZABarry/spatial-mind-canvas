# Research plan — solo spatial ideation vs desktop mind mapping

## Purpose

Validate whether **solo spatial ideation** in Spatial Mind Canvas is **meaningfully better** than desktop-only mind mapping for a defined target audience — before broadening product scope.

## Target users (hypothesis)

- Knowledge workers who already use diagrams or mind maps for structuring ideas.
- People willing to use a Quest-class headset for **focused** ideation sessions (not all-day replacement for 2D tools).

## Hypotheses to test

1. **Structure:** Users build clearer parent/child/link structure in 3D than in a comparable 2D tool in the same time box.
2. **Comfort:** The calm, local-first UX reduces anxiety about “getting it wrong” vs cloud tools.
3. **Flow:** Spatial navigation + recenter/undo supports longer uninterrupted sessions without losing context.

## What success looks like (directional)

- Participants report **higher confidence** in the map they built in VR vs 2D for the same prompt.
- **Time to first meaningful map** is comparable or better in VR after a short onboarding.
- **Repeat-use intent** is positive for at least a subset of participants.

## Ethics / logistics

- Solo sessions; no PII in repo artifacts.
- Keep sessions under 45 minutes; offer breaks in XR.
